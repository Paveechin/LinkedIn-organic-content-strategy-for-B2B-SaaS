## Post 2 - June 6, 2026
**Topic:** 7 lead magnet mistakes quietly killing your B2B pipeline

Most B2B Marketers do not have a traffic problem. They have a conversion problem hiding behind busy dashboards and polite silence from buyers.

1. You don't have a lead magnet

I learned this the hard way with B2B Marketers who kept asking why pipeline felt thin. No clear lead magnet meant no reason to raise a hand. Traffic came. Intent never did.

2. The offer is too generic

Classic pipeline killer. When the asset could fit any company in any market, it felt safe internally and invisible externally. Broad relevance produced weak leads and weaker follow up.

3. No follow up or nurture

I watched teams celebrate downloads that never turned into meetings. The magnet captured real intent. Engagement looked healthy. Revenue stayed painfully flat because the team never followed up.

4. The landing page asks for too much

More fields do not create better leads. They create friction, hesitation, and abandonment. Interest dies fast when a simple download feels like a procurement process.

5. The follow up is generic

We learned this after seeing strong opt in rates go nowhere. A bland nurture sequence treated every lead the same. Warm interest cooled off before sales ever had a chance.

6. There is no path to the next step

A lead magnet without progression is just content with a form on it. It collects names, then strands them. Momentum disappears when there is no obvious next move.

7. The funnel is never refined

I have seen decent funnels stay mediocre for months because no one touched the headline, page, or emails. Small leaks became expensive habits. Pipeline suffered in silence.

I made peace with one uncomfortable truth. Most lead funnel problems are not dramatic. They are quiet, cumulative, and brutal because they look normal until the quarter ends.

That is why getting the basics right matters so much. A simple funnel built with intent will outperform a complicated one built on assumptions.
