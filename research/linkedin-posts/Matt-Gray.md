## Post 1 - June 8, 2026
**Topic:** The 9 most powerful life hacks i've discovered

1. Surround Yourself With Optimists

Who you spend time with has a bigger influence on you than you know.

You're the average of the 5 people you spend the most time with.

Each of those 5 should be someone pushing you forward.

I used to tolerate energy vampires.

Then I realized: their mindset was becoming mine.

2. Write Every Day

Writing isn't just for writers.

It's for thinkers.

Writing builds discipline. 
Improves your communication. 
Organizes your thinking.

I write every single day.

Some days it's one sentence. Some days it's ten pages.

What matters is the habit.

3. Build a Personal Board of Advisors

Don't underestimate the power of a mentor.

Epictetus mentored Marcus Aurelius.

Jobs mentored Zuckerberg.

Seeking advice from people 2-3 steps ahead of you gives you access to their lifetime of wisdom in 2-3 years.

4. Sun Exposure Every Morning

Get sunlight in your eyes every morning.

It sounds simple. It is simple.

But it's powerful.

Sunlight sets your circadian rhythm. 
Primes your brain to be alert and focused. 
Enhances your metabolism and immune function.

No phone. No distractions.

Just 10 minutes in the sun.

5. Block Off Time to Read

The most successful people in the world have one thing in common:

They love to read.

I read every day. About things that interest me. And I re-read my favorite books.

An hour a day of reading puts you in the top 0.01% of people.

6. Meditate Daily

"All of man's problems come because he cannot sit by himself in a room for 30 minutes."

Our society is filled with cheap dopamine. 
Constant notifications. 
Screens everywhere.

Meditation is the antidote.

I meditate for 20 minutes every morning.

It's about learning to be present.

7. Set a Bedtime Alarm

Everyone has a morning alarm.

Almost no one has a bedtime alarm.

After this alarm goes off, I allow my mind to relax:

Turn off all devices. 
Take a warm shower or bath. 
Read my favorite fiction book.

Setting yourself up for success the next day starts the night before.

8. Practice Gratitude

Gratitude is a key ingredient for living a fulfilling life.

Gratitude helps people improve their health, feel more positive emotions, and build strong relationships.

I take 5 minutes every day to write down 5 things I'm grateful for.

It rewires your brain to see opportunity instead of obstacles.

9. Invest in Yourself

We spend 8 hours a day working for someone else.

But we won't take 30 minutes to work on ourselves.

I invest in myself through reading, exercising, and learning new skills.

Just 30 minutes a day can change your life.

The best investment you'll ever make is in yourself.

## Post 2 - June 7, 2026
**Topic:** Most founders build offers that solve the wrong problem


That's why they keep getting objections.

Here's the framework that changes everything:

There are 4 levels to every offer.

Most people never get past Level 1.

Level 1: Symptom Relief

This is what clients ask for when they first reach out.

They want the pain to stop. Right now.

Most offers live and die here.

The relief is temporary. They'll need you again next month.

Level 2: Root Cause

You're fixing the thing that created all the symptoms in the first place.

Clients don't always know this is what they need.

But it's the only way to create lasting change.

Dig into the root cause and your offer starts to feel different from everyone else's.

Level 3: Identity Shift

They're not just solving a problem anymore.

They're becoming the kind of person who doesn't have this problem.

Transformation happens at this level.

Level 4: Worldview Change

This means changing an entire operating system for how they think about their business.

They can't unsee what you've shown them.

They look at their old way of doing things and realize they were leaving everything on the table.

Most founders build offers at Level 1.

Tactics for 30 days and hope it works.

Irresistible offers hit all 4 levels.

I call this Outcome Stacking.

Here's what it looks like in practice:

• 30 days: Quick win: immediate results, momentum begins.
• 90 days: Systems start compounding: the team figures things out on their own.
• 6 months: Transformation: the business runs differently.
• 12+ months: New identity: operating at a completely different level, with more time.

Each outcome builds on the previous one.

Creates inevitable progression.

Makes the result feel achievable before they even start.

The formula is simple:

Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood = Value.

Time Delay + Effort = the enemy.

Maximize the first two.
Minimize the last two.

When you structure your offer this way, price becomes irrelevant.

The founders with the best offers have the clearest path from where their client is, to where they want to be.

Start there.

## Post 3 - June 6, 2026
**Topic:** Notebooks are the new operating systems

I know that sounds surprising, but hear me out.

Most founders have 17 half-filled notebooks scattered across their desk, car, and backpack.

Each one started with good intentions. Each one abandoned by week two.

Last year, I threw out every productivity app and went back to paper.

I built what I call "The Daily OS" a one-page framework that actually works.

Here's how to organize a notebook you'll actually use:

1. Theme Of The Day 

Start every day by naming what it's about.

Not a to-do list. An intention.

"Today is about deep creative work."
"Today is about strategic planning."
"Today is about connecting with my team."

This single sentence filters every decision you make.

2. Top 7 Priorities

Not 20 tasks. 
Not 50 emails. 
Seven priorities.

If you can't pick seven, you don't have clarity yet.

List them by impact, not urgency.

3. Meetings / Leverage Calls 

Here's the filter: Does this meeting create leverage or consume time?

If it doesn't compound, decline it or delegate it.

Only write down the calls that actually move needles.

4. Notes / Insights

Throughout the day, capture insights immediately.

Don't trust your memory. 

Write it down in the moment.

This section becomes your second brain.

5. The Scoreboard

Track three metrics daily:

• Gratitude (1 thing you're grateful for)
• Energy (1-10 scale, where 10 = you feel unbelievable)
• Revenue (1-10 scale, where 10 = you made significant progress)

Numbers don't lie. 

This scoreboard reveals patterns you'd otherwise miss.

6. End Of Day Reflection

Ask yourself 3 questions every evening:

What worked?
What didn't?
What will I accomplish tomorrow?

This 2-minute practice compounds into massive clarity over time.

The entire system fits on one page.

No apps. 
No syncing. 
No notifications.

Just a notebook and a pen.

Why does this work when digital tools fail?

• Because writing activates your brain differently than typing
• Because paper doesn't have notifications competing for your attention
• Because you can't optimize what you don't track, and this forces you to track what matters
