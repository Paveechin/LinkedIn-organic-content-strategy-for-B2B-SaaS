**Topic:** Everyone's working for the cheat code, and the cheat code is hard work

- Phase 1: Stay alive (Years 1-2)

## Post 3 - June 6, 2026
**Topic:** The happiest people I know all made a choice that looked unreasonable at some point

They quit a great job.
They turned down a big promotion.
They packed up and moved without a plan.

They ignored the reasonable choice the status quo expects you to make.

And they were almost always better off for it.

Most people always make the choice that looks logical.

The most "reasonable" one.

Take the promotion.
Move closer to the office.
Say yes to that bigger house.

Of course, there's nothing wrong with that.

And nobody could fault you for being reasonable.

But each time you make a choice like that, it makes the next one even easier to make.

Just another little reasonable link in your reasonable chain.

And those links add up.

Then one day, maybe 10 or 15 or 20 years later, you wake up inside a life you never actually chose.

It was built on autopilot.
One little reasonable decision at a time.

The people who break the chain?

They stop and ask one completely different question.

I wrote about it in this week's Saturday Essay.

It's called Unreasonable — Why choices that look wrong build lives that feel right.
